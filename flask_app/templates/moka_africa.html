Recipe for African Coffee On a 6 Cup Moka Pot

Water Temp : 208f
Coffee Amount : 22g
Water Amount : 345g
Brew Time : 3 min

Step One

Grind about 20-22 grams of coffee finer than you would for a pour over, but not quite as fine as you might for a true espresso machine—slightly larger than granulated sugar.

Step Two

Fill the bottom chamber of the moka pot with water until it is level with the valve, about 345g.

Step Three

Place the funnel "the coffee grounds receptacle" into the pot. If any water enters the funnel, pour out the excess and replace the funnel.

Step Four

Fill the funnel with the ground coffee, leveling the grounds and wiping the funnel’s rim clean. (Do not tamp the grounds).

Step Five

Screw the moka pot’s spouted top on tightly.

Step Six

Place the moka pot on a stove over medium heat. If using a gas stove, make sure the flame is not larger than the base of the pot so as not to expose the handle to heat.

Step Seven

As the water boils, the pressure will push the coffee into the upper chamber. You know it’s done when you hear a gurgling sound. Immediately remove the moka pot from the heat. Let the coffee finish flowing into the upper chamber.

Step Eight

-Serve and Enjoy.
