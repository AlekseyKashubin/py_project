Recipe for South American Coffee On V60

Water Temp : 205f
Coffee Amount : 24g
Water Amount : 390g
Brew Time : 3:30 min

Step One

Start by filling up your kettle and setting it to 205f.

Step Two

Rinse the paper filter with the now boiled water, ensuring that it is fully saturated.

Step Three

Grind your 24g of coffee to a medium consistency.

Step Four

Add 24g of ground coffee to the V60. With your finger, create a small well in the middle of the grounds.

Step Five

Start the timer and add 60g of water to bloom the coffee. Pick up and swirl the coffee 5 times.

Step Six

At the 45 second mark, continue pouring until the scale reads 300g. Aim to complete this pour within 30 seconds, finishing at the 1:15 mark. (Hoffman recommends a flow rate of 8g per second if possible.)

Step Seven

At the 1:15 minute mark, complete the pour by pouring until the scale reads 390g.

Step Eight

At the 1:30 minute mark, use the spoon to stir once clockwise and once anti-clockwise, knocking off the grounds from the sides of the V60.

Step Nine

After letting the V60 drain a little, give it 3 more swirls.

Step Ten

Allow the water to drip through the grounds entirely, ideally finishing by the 3:30 mark.

Step Eleven

Remove the filter and serve.
