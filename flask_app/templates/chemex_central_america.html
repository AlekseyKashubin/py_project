Recipe for Central American Coffee On Chemex

Water Temp : 205f
Coffee Amount : 48g
Water Amount : 700g
Brew Time : 4 min

Step One

Weigh out the coffee and grind it Coarse. We recommend using 48 grams of coffee and 700 grams of water (about 25 ounces), and then adjust according to your taste.

Step Two

Unfold your filter and place it in your Chemex, ensuring that the triple-fold portion is facing the pour spout. Fully saturate the filter and warm the vessel with hot water. Discard this water.

Step Three

Pour your ground coffee into the filter and give it a gentle shake. This will flatten the bed, allowing for a more-even pour.

Step Four

Starting at the bed’s center, gently pour 100g into your grounds. Work your way gently outward, You’ll notice that adding this amount of water causes the coffee to expand, or “bloom.” Allow it to do so for 45–55 seconds.

Step Five

Pour water starting in the center working toward the edge before spiraling back toward the middle. You should use about 200g of water for this pour.

Step Six

Repeat the same pour as in Step 5, adding water in 200g increments. Repeat once more, allowing the water to percolate through the grounds until the slurry drops 1 inch from the bottom of the filter before beginning the next pour.

Step Seven

Allow the water to drip through the grounds entirely. The brew should have taken between 3.5–4.5 minutes. Serve and Enjoy!
